Before we move into our discovery, let's have a more clear understanding of what each dog stage means:¶

1. Pupper: "Pupper" is a colloquial term used to refer to a small or young dog, typically a puppy. It is often associated with dogs in their early stages of life, characterized by their playful and adorable nature.

2. Doggo: "Doggo" is another slang term used to refer to a dog, typically in a more general sense. It can be used to describe any dog, regardless of age or size. "Doggo" is often used humorously or affectionately in internet memes and social media.

3. Puppo: "Puppo" is an endearing term used to refer to a dog, specifically emphasizing its cuteness or puppy-like qualities. It is similar to "pupper" but with a more affectionate tone, often used to describe dogs that are particularly adorable or charming.

4. Floofer: "Floofer" is a term used to describe a dog with long, fluffy fur or hair. It refers to dogs that have abundant, often voluminous, coats of fur that give them a fluffy and huggable appearance. Primarily used to describe a dog based on its physical characteristic of having a thick and fluffy coat.

It's important to note that these terms originated as internet slang and are not widely recognized in formal or official contexts. They have gained popularity through memes, social media, and online dog communities as a way to humorously and affectionately describe different aspects of dogs.¶
